# The 5 minutes before your alarm

Layer 1 / Awareness A example — shows what `/script-from-idea` produces from the example pseudo-script. Note the voice preservation (user's phrasings kept) and the three hook variations for A/B testing.

---

## HOOK (0–3 sec) — 3 variations

### A — Pseudo-script verbatim (recommended)

> [!example] Shot
> **Location:** Bed, phone on nightstand, before dawn
> **Action:** Camera on the bed at an angle. Phone visible on the nightstand with the alarm showing 5:55 AM. Hand reaches for the phone as you say the line.

"everyone talks about their morning routine."

**On-screen:** MORNING ROUTINE?

### B — Pointed

> [!example] Shot
> **Location:** Bathroom counter, morning light
> **Action:** Splash water on your face, look at yourself in the mirror on the last word.

"the real routine starts 5 minutes before your alarm."

**On-screen:** 5 MINUTES BEFORE

### C — Observational

> [!example] Shot
> **Location:** Sitting on the edge of the bed
> **Action:** Phone in hand, alarm ringing. Dismiss it. Look at camera.

"5 minutes from now decides your whole day. and you're not even awake."

**On-screen:** 5 MINUTES DECIDES

---

## ANCHOR (3–6 sec)

> [!example] Shot
> **Location:** Kitchen counter, making coffee
> **Action:** Pour coffee, look up to camera.

"everyone's got advice for the routine itself. nobody talks about the window before it."

---

## BODY (6–25 sec)

### 1 — The visible routine

> [!example] Shot
> **Location:** B-roll montage — smoothie blender, meditation cushion, running shoes by door
> **Action:** Quick cuts. Voiceover.

"what they eat.
what they drink.
when they meditate."

**On-screen:** THE ROUTINE

### 2 — The invisible decision

> [!example] Shot
> **Location:** Back on the bed, pre-dawn
> **Action:** Static shot. You're sitting up but haven't moved yet.

"nobody talks about the 5 minutes before the alarm.
the 5 minutes where you decide if you even get up."

**On-screen:** 5 MINUTES

---

## CTA (25–35 sec) — Layer 1: no call, end on insight

> [!example] Shot
> **Location:** Final shot of yourself at the kitchen counter with coffee
> **Action:** Look at the camera. Half-smile. Hold silence.

"that's the routine.
the rest is execution."

**On-screen:** THAT'S THE ROUTINE

### CTA alt (silent close)

> [!example] Shot
> **Location:** Same
> **Action:** No dialogue. Hold on the kitchen counter shot for 2 full seconds before cut.

(no dialogue)

---

## Format / filming notes

- Yap with b-roll inserts. Talking-head for hook + CTA, b-roll for body 1 montage.
- Energy: low volume, thoughtful. Don't sell this.
- Lighting: natural morning. Film the pre-dawn bed shots during actual pre-dawn — the blue-hour color grade is the visual signature.

## Retention devices

- The "5 minutes" phrase recurs 4 times — becomes the ear-worm.
- Contrast between the colorful visible routine (body 1 montage) and the static invisible decision (body 2) is the visual thesis.
- Closing on "the rest is execution" is a take that sounds right but is harder to apply — viewers pause to think, which is good for the algorithm.

## Pattern interrupts (timestamps)

- ~5s (bed → kitchen anchor)
- ~12s (kitchen → b-roll montage)
- ~20s (montage → back to bed, static shot)
- ~28s (bed → final kitchen shot)

## A/B test suggestion

Hook A (pseudo-script verbatim, wide observation) vs Hook C (stat framing, direct callout). Same body. A rewards the viewer who thinks they're about to hear generic advice; C rewards the viewer who wants a sharp take up front.
