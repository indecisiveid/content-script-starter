# Content Guide

## Content Strategy

### The Unifying Mental Model (Funnel + Awareness + Pillar + Format)

- **Funnel:** Why does this video exist? (Decides depth and CTA behavior)
- **Awareness:** Who is this written for — what do they already know? (Decides hook + tone)
- **Pillar / Angle:** How does it feel / what role does it play for the brand? (Decides message)
- **Format:** How hard is it to make + how will it be filmed? (Decides effort)

### Funnel Weekly Guide

- **Layer 1 - Face Familiarity (Top of Funnel): 40–50%**
- **Layer 2 - Belief Alignment (Core Messaging): 25–30%**
- **Layer 3 - Free Value (This is what I did): 15–20%**
- **Layer 4 - Demonstration / Proof (How {{BRAND}} works): 10–15%**
- **Layer 5 - Offer / Conversion (Sell it): <5%**

### Awareness vs Funnel — the two axes

These are different things, often conflated. Both live on every video.

- **Funnel layer** = *how much of this do I post?* — content distribution mix (Layer 1–5)
- **Awareness level** = *who is this written for?* — viewer mental state (Schwartz's A–E)

| Awareness | Viewer state | What the video does |
|---|---|---|
| A — Unaware | Doesn't know they have a problem | Pattern interrupt. Plant the seed. No problem language. |
| B — Problem-Aware | Knows the pain, no solution | Name it sharply. Agitate. No product. |
| C — Solution-Aware | Knows solutions exist, hasn't picked | Compare approaches. Why most fail. |
| D — Product-Aware | Knows {{BRAND}}, not convinced | Proof, demos, specifics. |
| E — Most Aware | Ready to buy, needs the trigger | Direct ask, urgency, offer. |

Funnel layer constrains CTA and product mention. Awareness level constrains hook and body framing. Full calibration matrix: see `Marketing AI System.md` → "Awareness level calibration".

## Layer 1 — Face Familiarity (40–50%)

**Goal:** Put your face on people's phones. NOT to teach, NOT to convert, NOT to explain {{BRAND}}.

**Script style:** Statements, not lessons.
- "Everyone talks about motivation. Nobody talks about what happens after week two."
- "I don't struggle with discipline. I struggle with starting."
- "Consistency sounds boring until you don't have it."

No CTA. No app mention. Filming: walking, coffee line, gym entrance, packing boxes, sitting in the car.

## Layer 2 — Belief Alignment (25–30%)

**Goal:** Make people think "Wait… that's exactly how I feel."

**Script style:** Declarative. Opinionated. Sharp.
- "Outcome goals are why accountability fails."
- "Discipline isn't rare. Systems are."
- "If willpower worked, alarms would've fixed it."

Still no product pitch. CTA: soft positioning only.

## Layer 3 — Free Value (15–20%)

**Goal:** Prove you're practical, not just philosophical. Triggers saves and replays.

**Framing:** Not "Here's how YOU should do it" → Instead "Here's what worked for ME"
- "This is what I changed when I stopped quitting after two weeks."
- "This is how I stopped negotiating with myself in the morning."

## Layer 4 — Demonstration / Proof (10–15%)

**Goal:** Make the abstract concrete. {{BRAND}} starts to appear — but quietly.

Content types: POV routines, Tapping checkpoints, Missed days, Streaks, "Almost quit today" moments.
- "If I don't do this, the alarm comes back on."

Still no hard sell.

## Layer 5 — Offer / Conversion (<5%)

**Goal:** Give people a place to go after they already want it.

Only activate when: People ask where to get it, People explain it to each other in comments, Core messaging lands consistently.

Framing: "This is the system I built because nothing else worked for me."

## How to Post 3x/Day

**Post 1 (AM):** Low-lift / motivational / relatable
**Post 2 (Midday):** Day X / system / behind the scenes
**Post 3 (PM):** Trend-based or hot take

Luxury videos replace one slot on those days.

## Day X Prompt Bank

1. "What surprised me most about being consistent"
2. "The hardest part of today's routine"
3. "Why today almost didn't happen"
4. "One mistake I made early"
5. "What I used to believe about motivation"
6. "What happens when I miss a day"
7. "The mental trick that helps me start"
8. "Why tracking habits never worked for me"
9. "What consistency actually feels like"
10. "The difference between routines and goals"
11. "Why alarms alone don't work"
12. "What happens if I bail"
13. "How long it took before this felt normal"
14. "Why I stopped negotiating with myself"
15. "The friction that actually helps"
16. "How my energy changed after 2 weeks"
17. "Why I don't rely on discipline"
18. "What I do on low-motivation days"
19. "What this system removes from my brain"
20. "Who this won't work for (and that's okay)"

## Scheduling Strategy

Pillar balance:
- Motivation: ~25%
- Consistency / Systems: ~30%
- Proof / BTS: ~20%
- Hot takes / Anti-advice: ~15%
- Luxury: ~10%

### Monthly Strategy Lock (30–45 min, once per month)
- Primary goal (pick ONE): App installs, Social proof/trust, or Habit education
- Secondary: Engagement or Creator-style reach

### Creator Insights Sweep (20–30 min, 2x per week)
- TikTok Creator Insights → Trending Topics → Filter: Lifestyle, Productivity, Fitness, Self-improvement
- Log: Trend, Audience, Hook Pattern, Format, Gap I Can Fill, {{BRAND}} Tie-In

### Weekly Content Assembly (60 min)
- 14–18 total videos: 10–12 low-lift, 4–6 yap, 2 luxury
- Script hooks + beats, not full scripts

### Batch Recording (2–3 hours)
- Batch by format, not topic
- Film all yap → all b-roll → luxury (separate energy)

### Daily Posting + Feedback Loop (10 min/day)
- Post → Screenshot retention graph, saves, comments
- Add 1 sentence: "People commented most on ___" or "Drop-off at ___ seconds"

## Posting Tips

- Use Creator Search Insights to find trending topics and upload under them
- Add captions and filter directly within TikTok
- Generate automatic subtitles and move them out of the way

## Hook Tips

- Talk less about myself and more about the emotion/event the viewer can relate to
- First 3 seconds grabs attention — start with what matters to the viewer
- Seconds 3–6: Attention anchor with curiosity gap (stakes or social proof)
- State the outcome before the explanation

## Script Structure

Every script has 4 sections:
1. **Hook** — Verbal, Written, Visual
2. **Value** — Using own experience, decide what to teach
3. **CTA** — "If you're a lazy bum like me, get your checkpoints at the link in my bio"
4. **Format** — Choose how to film

## Rules from Learnings

- Don't make a video longer than 30 seconds unless necessary
- Remove all Um's
- Keep a strong cadence
- Start sentences with the result, then explain, then intro the solution
- Don't add sentences that don't add value
- Title and beginning of video should be different
- Add visuals to the things you talk about
- Have a clear visual hook at the beginning
- Keep video well lit
